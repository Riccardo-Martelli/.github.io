Integral 7

\begin{equation} I=\int_0^\infty \frac{\ln(x^2+c^2)}{x^{2}+b^{2}}\,dx\, , \end{equation} For this integral we can consider \(f(z) = \frac{\ln(z+ic)}{z^{2}+b^{2}}\), we have that \[\lim_{R\to \infty} \int_{\Gamma_R} \to 0\,,\quad \lim_{\varepsilon\to 0}\int_{\epsilon_0} \to 0\,,\] than \[2\pi i \Res[f(z)]_{z=ib} = 2\pi i \frac{\ln(i(\abs{c}+\abs{b}))}{2i\abs{b}}= \frac{\pi}{\abs{b}} (\ln{(\abs{c}+\abs{b})}+i\arg(i(\abs{c}+\abs{b})))\] The integral on the positive real line is trivial, the one on the negative real line is quite important \[\int_{\mathbb{R}^-} \frac{\ln(z+i\abs{c})+i\pi}{z^{2}+b^{2}}\, dz \stackrel{z\to-z}{=} \int_{\mathbb{R}^+} \frac{\ln(-z+i\abs{c})}{z^{2}+b^{2}}\, dz + i \frac{\pi^2}{2\abs{b}}\] Putting all together we have \[I + i \frac{\pi^2}{2\abs{b}} = i \frac{\pi^2}{2\abs{b}} + \frac{\pi}{\abs{b}}\ln{(\abs{c}+\abs{b})}\,,\] \[I = \frac{\pi}{\abs{b}}\ln{(\abs{c}+\abs{b})}\,.\] \(\qed\)
